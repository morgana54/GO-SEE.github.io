What do girls who are never asked for their opinion have to say? How do they struggle to grow in the fashion industry that is both beautiful and ruthless at the same time? 8 models will speak up about how they matured in such a demanding and peculiar environment. Extremely intimate, unique meetings and excerpts from everyday life of the models in Tokyo create together a touching story about growing up away from home. The life of the models in this dynamic and colorful city, interspersed with their often-controversial statements, creates an honest portrayal of the fashion world, which is itself full of contrasts.

ABOUT THE FILM

Shot in Japan and Poland, the documentary by Julia Kuzka is not only a coming of age story about a great adventure, but also about loneliness and other challenges that the models face every day. Apart from the models, the film will also feature their parents and photographers, who will talk about the modeling industry from their perspective. Extremely intimate, sincere and often controversial statements, combined with snippets from the everyday life of the models, constitute a beautiful and honest picture of the fashion world. The documentary focuses on the psychological aspect of modeling work, the difficulties of travelling alone and fighting the temptations of the Tokyo night life, which are hard to resist...
The film is addressed to all those who have been fascinated by the world of fashion since their childhood, and also to those who might appear as having nothing to do with the fashion industry whatsoever. That’s because "GO&SEE" is above all a story about growing up in an environment where the value of a person is defined by their physicality. This, as one knows, does not only apply to this profession... We hope to make the public reflect on the ethics of the another side of the fashion world and on the values that we often unconsciously follow nowadays. True - this is what "GO&SEE" will be like.

MISSION
From the very beginning of our work on the film, our greatest goal has been to draw the attention of the general public to hitherto unknown aspects of the models' work. Many people working in the production, including the initiator and director Julia Kuzka, have many years of experience in the modeling industry, therefore producing and releasing "GO&SEE" is a personal mission for all us. We wish to show the true face of the models' work in order to confront common beliefs about this profession as easy, pleasant or prestigious at all times. We do not want to impose any filters on the material shown, in order to honestly reflect both the beautiful and daunting sides of modeling.
It is our dream to create a kind of movement that will support all those who want to or already do work in the modeling industry. In particular, girls who are aspiring to start their adventure with modeling - often very young and unsupported by their parents. Furthermore, we want to reach out to beginner models as well as the experienced ones, that are daily confronted with incomprehension, loneliness and lack of self-apprehension.
"GO&SEE" is created in an intimate atmosphere thanks to the work of motivated, creative and talented people, for whom this film reflects the personal need to give a voice to those who are never asked for their opinions.
STRIKE A POSE
STRIKE
A POSE
Long before you will get to see your face on the glossy magazine covers sold in street kiosks or the commercial banners for shique boutiques, You'll have to walk through countless castings, photo studios, make up schools and designer ateliers while also living your life through a handful of countries outside your native continent. Nobody said it’s gonna be easy - If you’re lucky you’ll earn some money after that second contract you had in Asia, which will help you wipe the tears of frustration and demotivation. You have to remember to be strong, self confident and patient. Don’t forget to smile, even when your 16 year-old contract-bound roommate from East Europe is driving you crazy. Clients don’t like pout models, right? Probably you won’t even get this job you so long awaited cause of another 16 year old girl from east Europe - she's been in the business, at least, two years longer than you - her portfolio is bigger and her hips are smaller. Just don’t give up, cause, eventually, someone might give you a chance. At that point, all you have to do is strike a pose.
I started working on Strike a Pose project in August 2018 during my first model trip to Seoul. Since that time I’ve been staying in China, Japan and Europe, photographing life of models on the contract abroad. For 2 years I took hundreds of moments from the backstage of, looking like luxurious, fashion industry. As in my every project I’m focus on autobiographical context which is showed by people surrounding me. In the same time I'm letting my viewers look behind the curtain, open another closet. I'm showing the world of expensive brands from the undergrund, step further than instagram- there nobody wants to share that your fancy models apartment is not fancy at all. Strike a Pose project is on going.
Alicja Łabądź
ABOUT US
The "GO&SEE" project was initially carried out by one person, who really believed it is the high time to tell the story of the models working on contracts in Tokyo. In fact, she herself was one of them at the time of shooting. Today, a team of creative and highly motivated people are working on the project, aiming to make the document see the light of day.
